Why this matters at Kretz

Many useful processes live in people's heads, old emails, or informal routines. AI can help turn that knowledge into draft SOPs, checklists, and training references, but only after the process owner verifies the steps.

Open Kretz AI Decision Guide Open regular ChatGPT

Step 1: Pick a process

Choose one real repeated process, such as landowner call intake, customer inquiry routing, a safety talk process, haul planning notes, office marketing requests, or meeting follow-up.

Prompt

Ask me questions to help document a Kretz process as a reusable SOP or checklist. Start by asking what the process is, who owns it, what triggers it, what systems are used, and what the final output should be.

Step 2: Turn plain language into structure

Describe the process like you would explain it to a new employee. Then ask AI to organize it.

Structured prompt

You are helping create a draft Kretz process document. Turn the notes below into a practical SOP/checklist. Include: purpose, owner, when to use it, required inputs, step-by-step process, review points, common mistakes, what not to enter into public AI tools, and who approves changes. Notes: [paste notes here]

Step 3: Use the Decision Guide

Paste the process idea into the Kretz AI Decision Guide and ask whether this should be a Custom GPT, existing software setup, automation, process cleanup, or vendor system.

Decision Guide prompt

We are thinking about using AI to support this process. Ask questions and tell us whether this should be AI, existing software, automation, process improvement, a vendor system, or no AI for now: [paste process]

Governance check

  • Who owns the process?
  • What data is sensitive?
  • Who reviews the AI-created draft?
  • Where will the final approved SOP live?
  • How often should it be reviewed?
