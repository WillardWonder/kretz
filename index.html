AI News Break.

Use these as short, high-energy cautionary moments inside the presentation. Each one connects directly to a governance lesson.

AI News Break

The chatbot became the company voice.

Customer-facing AI can give wrong answers that still create real business consequences.

Lesson: AI that talks to customers needs approval, testing, and review.
AI News Break

Confident output, fake information.

AI can sound polished and authoritative while generating inaccurate facts, sources, or recommendations.

Lesson: High-risk outputs require human verification.
AI News Break

Employees used public AI before policy existed.

Without clear guidance, employees may enter sensitive information into tools the organization does not control.

Lesson: Governance needs to come before uncontrolled adoption.
AI News Break

A polished demo failed in the real world.

AI pilots can work in theory but fail when they meet messy operations, edge cases, and real customers.

Lesson: Pilot carefully, measure results, and keep humans accountable.

What happens next.

The end of the training should move from excitement to responsible action. These are the building blocks for a practical AI path forward.

Develop governance policyDefine what employees can use AI for, what data is protected, and when human review is required.
Select approved toolsChoose platforms that fit the organization's security, cost, workflow, and training needs.
Define teams and ownershipClarify who owns pilots, review, maintenance, training, security, and business outcomes.
Build and maintain solutionsTreat AI tools as living systems that need testing, feedback, updates, and process support.
Start with useful pilotsFocus on repeated, valuable, manageable workflows before moving into higher-risk use cases.
Protect sensitive informationSet clear rules for customer, employee, pricing, operational, financial, legal, and safety-related data.
Require human reviewAI can assist with drafts, summaries, and analysis, but people remain accountable for decisions and communication.
Stay adaptableThe AI landscape changes quickly. Tools, policies, and training must be reviewed regularly.
