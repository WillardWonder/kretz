Facilitator guide v3

Kretz AI Leadership and Governance Training

Updated to match the source-linked newsbreaks, cleaned-up Kretz prototypes, Custom GPT activities, and uploadable synthetic practice datasets.

Core session goal

Help Kretz leaders understand current AI tools, business impact, real risks, and the governance needed before adoption spreads. The session should feel practical and leadership-level, not technical.

Recommended 2-hour flow

TimeSegmentFacilitator move
0:00-0:10Welcome and framingExplain opportunity plus responsibility. AI is already available to employees, so leadership needs rules.
0:10-0:25What AI is and is notUse the simple mental model: AI produces plausible language, not guaranteed truth.
0:25-0:45AI News BreakUse 4-6 clickable examples. Emphasize one lesson per story: ownership, verification, data protection, guardrails.
0:45-1:05Kretz prototype demosShow Claude Parcel Finder first, then Landowner Prospecting, Timber Market Calculator, and Haul Planner if time allows.
1:05-1:20Kretz DNA AssistantPaste a rough landowner or customer message. Let the GPT rewrite it and flag review items. If access fails, use regular ChatGPT with the Kretz DNA prompt.
1:20-1:40Kretz AI Decision GuideHave participants describe a real problem. The GPT should decide AI, existing software, automation, process cleanup, vendor system, or no AI.
1:40-1:55Governance discussionDiscuss protected data, sign-off, no-fault reporting, approved tools, and ownership.
1:55-2:00CloseAsk each person to name one useful AI opportunity and one governance rule Kretz should set first.

Prototype demo order

  1. Claude Parcel Finder Example: one-shot coding example. Shows speed and risk.
  2. Landowner Prospecting Tool: conversationally refined tool. Shows better prompting/process.
  3. Timber Market Intelligence Calculator: Troy blog/company knowledge made interactive.
  4. Kretz Haul Planner: operational workflow layer and logistics discussion.

After every prototype ask: What data does this depend on? Where could it be wrong? Who reviews it? What rule would Kretz need before routine use?

Dataset-based activities

Use the synthetic dataset ZIP. Participants can upload one file into ChatGPT or a Custom GPT and ask for summaries, drafts, checklists, or risk reviews.

  • Landowner call notes: draft follow-up and review checklist.
  • Customer inquiries: avoid inventing price, availability, delivery, or commitments.
  • CRM/follow-up sample: decide CRM, existing software, automation, GPT, or process cleanup.
  • Vendor research starter: create comparison tables and vendor questions.
  • Meeting notes: summarize decisions, actions, open questions, and governance risks.
  • Kretz voice samples: identify tone and improve message rewriting.

Fallback plan if internet or GPT access fails

  1. Use screenshots or the downloaded PPTX only.
  2. Read the synthetic scenario aloud.
  3. Ask participants to choose the AI path manually using the decision framework slide.
  4. Paste the short GPT instructions into regular ChatGPT if shared GPT access is blocked.
  5. Keep the discussion focused on governance: data, accuracy, review, ownership.

Discussion prompts

  • Which Kretz workflows are repeated enough to justify a narrow GPT?
  • Which problems are actually Outlook, Teams, Planner, SharePoint, CRM, or process problems?
  • What data should never go into public AI tools?
  • Which outputs require sign-off before they leave the company?
  • Who should own AI tools after the demo is over?
