Use case

This activity adapts the old sourcing example into a Kretz-specific research workflow. Instead of product material sourcing, participants use AI to organize research around manufacturing, grading, forestry, CRM, or workflow technology.

Open Kretz AI Decision Guide Open regular ChatGPT

Step 1: Define the research question

Pick one area: grading technology, CRM/project tracking, landowner communication tools, safety training systems, office marketing tools, or manufacturing workflow systems.

Prompt

Help us research options for [technology/process area]. Start by asking what problem we are trying to solve, what systems we already use, who would use it, what data it needs, and what risks or integrations matter.

Step 2: Create a comparison table

Prompt

Create a vendor or solution comparison table with columns for: option, what problem it solves, likely users, data needed, integration questions, implementation complexity, risks, questions to ask the vendor, and what a pilot would look like. Do not invent pricing or product claims. Flag anything that needs source verification.

Step 3: Create a decision tree

Prompt

Create a practical decision tree that helps Kretz decide whether this should be handled with existing software, a workflow automation, a narrow Custom GPT, a vendor system, or process cleanup first.

Source and verification rule

AI can help organize research, but it should not be treated as the source of truth. Vendor claims, pricing, capabilities, integrations, security, and support should be verified through legitimate sources, vendor documentation, demos, references, and Kretz review.

Key takeaway

AI is useful for preparing better questions and comparison structures. It should not replace due diligence, vendor review, technical validation, or leadership decision-making.
